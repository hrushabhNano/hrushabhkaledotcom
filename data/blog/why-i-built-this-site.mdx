I've been building things on the internet professionally for four years. Enterprise apps. Client portals. AI assistants. WhatsApp bots that handle more messages in a day than I've sent in my life. And until now, none of it lived at a URL with my name on it.

That's a strange thing to sit with.

The honest reason I didn't build this earlier is that I kept framing it as something I had to get right before I shipped it. The design had to be perfect. The writing had to be good. The projects section couldn't just list things — it had to have case studies. By the time I had talked myself into and out of it enough times, a year had passed and the Notion document with "portfolio ideas" was still empty.

The useful thing that broke the loop was realising the site wasn't really for potential clients or recruiters. I have a job I like. I'm not looking. The site is for me — a place where the work I do is visible to anyone who looks up my name, rather than living inside five different client portals and a few GitHub repos with no READMEs.

There's also a specific thing that changed in how I think about this. I'm at a stage in my career where I've accumulated enough opinions that I want to write them down. Not for SEO. Not to grow a following. Just because building complex systems over four years produces a lot of thinking that doesn't have anywhere to go, and writing is the best way I've found to figure out what I actually think about something.

Some of what I'll write here: how we're actually using AI at Nanostuffs (not the hype version), what I've learned about shipping React Native apps across multiple countries, why production Heroku architecture is more interesting than people give it credit for, and what technical leadership looks like in a small startup — the version where you're responsible for estimates, architecture, client calls, and mentoring all in the same afternoon.

Not a lot of the Indian tech creator content I see goes into this kind of specificity. Most of it is either motivational or surface-level. I want to write the posts I was looking for two years ago and couldn't find.

That's it. No grand vision for the site. I'll write when I have something to say, ship things when they're done enough, and update stuff when it gets embarrassingly out of date.

If you want to follow along — the blog is here. If you disagree with something I write, my email is in the footer.
